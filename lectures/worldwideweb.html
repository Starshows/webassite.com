1993

Marc Andreessen leaves NCSA to start the first commercial browser company, Netscape

1995

Microsoft, initially shunning the WWW, licenses Mosaic code and jumps in with Internet Explorer

1995

Netscape goes public in 1995 with the largest IPO in history, initially raising over $4bn, stabilizing at $2.9bn. Andreessen ends up on the cover of Time the following year.

1997

Microsoft launches IE 4, which finally reaches feature parity with Netscape.

1996-2009

Browser Market Share
Owning 90% of the PC market, Microsoft's bundling strategy kills Netscape

1998

Anti-Trust
Microsoft ends up in anti-trust court where a witness testifies that Bill Gates had set out to "cut off Netscape's air supply" in 1995. Microsoft beats the charges and browser innovation goes into the dark ages.

1998
Microsoft beat the anti-trust suit and browser innovation went into the dark ages. The competitive years between 1995-1998 left us with two core technologies/languages that continue to be the backbone of web development: CSS and Javascript.
